computeGravitationalAcceleration((object)universalGravitationalConstant, (Vector3d)positionOfBodySubjectToAcceleration, (object)massOfBodyExertingAcceleration, (Vector3d)positionOfBodyExertingAcceleration) → Vector3d :¶

Computes gravitational acceleration experienced by body1, due to its interaction with body2.

The basis for this gravitational acceleration is that body2 is a point mass, generating acceleration due to Newton’s gravitational force:

\[\bar{a}_{gravity} = -\frac{G * m_{2}}{r_{12}^{3}} * \bar{r}_{12}\]

where \(G\) is the universal gravitational constant, \(m_{2}\) is the mass of body2, and \(\bar{r}_{12}\) is the relative position vector from body1 to body 2, with respect to an inertial (barycentric) reference frame.

param universalGravitationalConstant:
 Universal gravitational constant [m^3 kg^-1 s^-2].
param positionOfBodySubjectToAcceleration:
 Position vector of body subject to acceleration (body1) [m].
param massOfBodyExertingAcceleration:
 Mass of body exerting acceleration (body2) [kg].
param positionOfBodyExertingAcceleration:
 Position vector of body exerting acceleration (body2) [m].
return:Gravitational acceleration exerted on body1 [m s^-2].

Computes gravitational acceleration experienced by body1, due to its interaction with body2.

The basis for this gravitational acceleration is that body2 is a point mass, generating acceleration due to Newton’s gravitational force:

\[\bar{a}_{gravity} = -\frac{\mu_{2}}{r_{12}^{3}} * \bar{r}_{12}\]

where \(\mu_{2}\) is the gravitational parameter of the body exerting acceleration, and \(\bar{r}_{12}\) is the relative position vector from body1 to body 2, with respect to an inertial (barycentric) reference frame.

param positionOfBodySubjectToAcceleration:
 Position vector of body subject to acceleration (body1) [m].
param gravitationalParameterOfBodyExertingAcceleration:
 Gravitational parameter of body exerting acceleration (body2) [m^3 s^-2].
param positionOfBodyExertingAcceleration:
 Position vector of body exerting acceleration (body2) [m].
return:Gravitational acceleration exerted on body1 [m s^-2].

computeGravitationalForce((object)universalGravitationalParameter, (object)massOfBodySubjectToForce, (Vector3d)positionOfBodySubjectToForce, (object)massOfBodyExertingForce, (Vector3d)positionOfBodyExertingForce) → Vector3d :¶

Computes gravitational force experienced by body1, due to its interaction with body2.

The basis for this gravitational force is that both body1 and body2 are point masses, generating Newton’s gravitational force:

\[\bar{F}_{gravity} = -\frac{G * m_{1} * m_{2}}{r_{12}^{3}} * \bar{r}_{12}\]

where \(G\) is the universal gravitational constant, \(m_{1}\) is the mass of body1, \(m_{2}\) is the mass of body2, and \(\bar{r}_{12}\) is the relative position vector from body1 to body 2, with respect to an inertial (barycentric) reference frame.

param universalGravitationalParameter:
 Universal gravitational constant [m^3 kg^-1 s^-2].
param massOfBodySubjectToForce:
 Mass of body subject to force (body1) [kg].
param positionOfBodySubjectToForce:
 Position vector of body subject to force (body1) [m].
param massOfBodyExertingForce:
 Mass of body exerting force (body2) [kg].
param positionOfBodyExertingForce:
 Position vector of body exerting force (body2) [m].
return:Gravitational force exerted on body1 [N].

Computes the energy of an orbiting body that follows a conic section (Kepler orbit). The default mass value is for the two-body orbital energy per unit mass. For closed conic sections (circles, ellipses), the semi-major axis is positive, and for open sections (hyperbolas) the semi-major axis is negative.
